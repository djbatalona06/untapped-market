## FACEBOOK POST

Every weed app tells you the same three things: relaxed, happy, creative. That description fits a yoga mat commercial, not a strain guide. 🌿

Here's what's actually behind that "couch-lock feeling" you've been blaming on indica genetics — it's not the genetics. It's a terpene called myrcene, and it's in more than 40% of cannabis strains at dominant levels.

Myrcene is earthy, musky, herbal. Think fresh hops from a Willamette Valley brewery, ripe mango in July, damp forest floor somewhere off Highway 2. It's also found in lemongrass, thyme, and hops — which is why your favorite IPA and your favorite strain hit different notes but share something underneath.

Here's the number that matters: 0.5%. Below that threshold, myrcene tends to run energetic and clear-headed. Above it, sedating. That's the real story behind couch-lock — not whether your flower came labeled indica or sativa.

The "eat a mango before you smoke" trick that's been floating around the PNW forever? That one's legitimate. Mangoes are loaded with myrcene. So is cannabis. Stacking them creates a real entourage effect — the compounds work together and move through the blood-brain barrier faster than either one alone. Your uncle wasn't wrong, he just didn't have the vocabulary.

In the Pacific Northwest, Rainier Kush and Willamette Valley OG carry myrcene as a dominant terpene. If you've smoked either one and wondered why the session felt heavy and full — now you know exactly what you were tasting. 🏔️
