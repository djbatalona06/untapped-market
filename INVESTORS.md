# Untapped Market — Investor Opportunities & Monetization Plan

> Strategic fundraising and revenue plan for **Untapped Market** — a PNW-first
> cannabis strain discovery, dispensary finder, and personal library app.
> Differentiated by genetic-lineage data, lab COA rigor, terpene profiles, and
> a consumer-product feel (think Letterboxd / Spotify for cannabis) versus
> Leafly and Weedmaps, which are dispensary marketing platforms.

---

## 1. Where Untapped Market sits in the market

| | Untapped Market | Leafly | Weedmaps | Dutchie / Jane |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Primary user | Consumer | Consumer (but ad-driven for dispensaries) | Dispensary directory | Dispensary (POS / e-com) |
| Core value | Genetics, terpenes, COAs, personal library | Strain encyclopedia + menus | Map + menus | Order infrastructure |
| Geography | PNW-first, depth over breadth | National | National | National |
| Business model | Consumer subscription + B2B data | Ad / lead-gen | Ad / SaaS for dispensaries | SaaS + transaction fees |

**Pitch in one line:** *"Weedmaps is Yelp for dispensaries. Untapped Market is
Letterboxd for what's actually in the jar — built on genetic data, lab COAs,
and the densest cultivar density in the US (the PNW)."*

## 3. Profitability plan — how Untapped Market actually makes money

The current $7/mo Premium tier is a starter. The defensible long-term margin
comes from layering **four** revenue streams on the same data flywheel.

### 3.1 Revenue stream summary

| # | Stream | Stage to launch | Margin profile | LTV character |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Consumer subscription (B2C) | Now (live) | High (~85%) | Mid; churn-sensitive |
| 2 | Dispensary lead-gen / affiliate (CPC/CPL) | Month 3–6 | High | Volume-driven |
| 3 | Dispensary B2B SaaS (analytics + menu widget) | Month 9–12 | Very high | Highest LTV |
| 4 | Data licensing to cultivators / brands / labs | Month 12–18 | Highest | Enterprise-style |

Together these turn the consumer app into a **two-sided data product**:
consumer demand on one side, dispensary/brand supply on the other.

### 3.2 Stream 1 — Consumer subscription (Premium)

**Today:** $7/mo or $63/yr Premium with smart folders, alerts, COA export,
genetic deep-dives, live inventory map, consumption log.

**Proposed expansion:**

- **Free** — strain catalog, dispensary finder, basic library, trip reports.
- **Premium $7/mo** *(unchanged)* — current feature set.
- **Pro $19/mo** *(new)* — adds:
  - Full COA history per strain (not just current batch)
  - Terpene-effect correlation reports for your saved library
  - Multi-region tracking (e.g., follow strains in both Seattle and Portland)
  - API key for personal use (data export to Notion, etc.)
  - Early access to new cultivar drops
- **Annual discount** — keep "$63/yr — 2 months free" framing on Premium;
  $190/yr on Pro.

**Conversion targets:**
- Free → Premium: 3–5% of MAU is realistic for a content + utility app.
- Premium → Pro: 10–15% of Premium users.

**Retention levers:**
- Saved strain alerts (utility hook — high stickiness)
- Consumption log streaks (habit hook)
- Personal taste profile that improves over time (data moat against switching)

### 3.3 Stream 2 — Lead-gen / affiliate from dispensaries

The Weedmaps playbook, but earned, not paid-only.

- **Click-to-menu deep links** — when a user taps "in stock at" on a strain
  detail page, that handoff is monetizable. CPC ($0.50–$2.00) or CPL
  (verified visit, $5–$20).
- **"Reserve at this dispensary"** — affiliate kickback on any pre-order
  completed via partner POS (Dutchie, Jane). Negotiable rev-share, typically
  3–8% of order value.
- **Promoted dispensaries** — dispensaries pay to be listed first within a ZIP
  radius. Must be clearly labeled "Sponsored" — App Store + trust both demand
  it.

**Estimated unit economics**
- 50K MAU → ~150K monthly menu-link clicks → at $0.75 average CPC = $112K /
  month *gross*. Even at 20% sell-through with launch dispensaries that's
  ~$22K/mo.

### 3.4 Stream 3 — Dispensary B2B SaaS (the moat)

This is the highest-LTV, hardest-to-displace stream and the one most cannabis
investors will get most excited about.

**Product: Untapped Market for Dispensaries** ($199–$799/mo tiered)

- **Demand dashboard** — what strains are users in *your* trade area saving,
  searching, alert-subscribing to. Heatmap by ZIP. Trend lines.
- **Stockout alerts** — your store appears in users' "back in stock" alerts;
  dashboard shows demand for items you don't carry.
- **Embeddable strain cards** — paste a snippet on the dispensary's own site
  to show terpene + COA + lineage data tied to their menu.
- **Competitor benchmark** — anonymized aggregate view of how your menu's
  genetic diversity / lab transparency compares to neighbors.
- **Customer arrival data** — verified-visit pings (opt-in by users) to prove
  app-driven foot traffic.

**Tiers**
- **Listed** — $199/mo — basic dashboard, demand heatmap, embed widget.
- **Plus** — $399/mo — adds competitor benchmarks, stockout alerts, monthly
  insights report.
- **Pro** — $799/mo — adds CSV / API data export, dedicated CSM, A/B menu
  tests.

**Target market math**
- Washington has ~500 active retail licenses. Oregon has ~700. PNW total
  ~1,200 dispensaries.
- 10% penetration at avg $400 = $480K MRR / $5.7M ARR from PNW alone before
  expansion.

### 3.5 Stream 4 — Data licensing to cultivators, brands, labs

The flywheel: every Premium user's saved-strain, alert, and consumption log
data (anonymized + aggregated) becomes a panel that nobody else has at the
genetic + terpene level of granularity.

- **Cultivator demand reports** — "Pheno X of GMO is 4× more bookmarked in
  Portland than Seattle this month." Sold quarterly to PNW cultivators
  ($2–10K per report, or $25–60K annual subscription).
- **Brand discovery reports** — emerging genetics by region, sold to MSOs and
  consumer brands ($50K+ annual).
- **Lab partner program** — testing labs pay a flat monthly fee ($99–299) for
  a "Verified by ___ Lab" badge on COA entries that came through their lab,
  driving brand awareness with end consumers.
- **Press / industry reports** — quarterly "State of PNW Cultivars" trend
  PDF — paid sponsorship slots from ancillary brands.

### 3.6 Stream 5 — Adjacencies (later)

- **Telehealth referrals** — partner with Heally, NuggMD, etc. for medical
  card sign-ups; rev-share per referral ($20–60).
- **Ancillary commerce affiliate** — storage, vapes, glass, scales,
  smell-proof bags. App Store-safe (no plant-touching). 8–15% commission.
- **Education / events** — paid masterclass video series, ticketed PNW
  cultivator tastings, sponsored "Strain of the Month" tours.
- **White-label** — license the strain detail viewer + COA renderer to
  dispensary websites and POS partners.

### 3.7 What we will *not* monetize (and why)

- **No selling individual user identities** — kills consumer trust in a
  privacy-sensitive vertical, and is App Store / GDPR risk.
- **No direct in-app cart / checkout for cannabis** — Apple App Store
  policies prohibit this. Discovery and deep-link handoff only.
- **No paid review placement** — we are the trust layer; this is the
  Letterboxd-not-Yelp rule.

## 4. Risk disclosures (be upfront with investors)

- **Federal Schedule I status.** Affects banking, payment processors, paid
  acquisition (Meta, Google ad bans on cannabis content), and App Store
  policies. Mitigation: our app is informational/discovery — we don't process
  cannabis transactions.
- **App Store policy risk.** Apple permits cannabis info apps but has
  rejected for borderline commerce features. Mitigation: deep-link to
  dispensary partner web flows; no in-app cart.
- **State-by-state regulation.** Each new market adds legal review overhead.
  Mitigation: PNW-first depth; expand after dominance.
- **Incumbents.** Leafly (~$26M revenue, public, distressed) and Weedmaps
  (~$200M revenue, public). Differentiation: consumer-product focus, genetic
  / COA depth, PNW-first content moat.
- **Cannabis VC market compression.** Capital availability has shrunk vs.
  2020–2021. Mitigation: capital-efficient plan, real revenue path, lean
  team.
- **Data sourcing.** COA scraping / lab partnerships need formal
  agreements. Mitigation: lab partner program (Stream 4) directly aligns
  incentives.

### 8.6 Outreach hygiene

A few rules to keep this list useful instead of burned:

- **Verify partner moves.** Cannabis VC has had heavy partner churn since
  2023. Re-confirm names against firm websites and LinkedIn the day you
  send.
- **Never paste the same email body to multiple firms in the same week.**
  Cannabis is a small world and partners forward decks. Tailor each.
- **Lead with traction, not vision.** Almost every firm above has been
  pitched the "Letterboxd for cannabis" angle by someone before. The
  hook that breaks through is real PNW MAU, real save-rate, real
  dispensary LOIs.
- **Don't pitch flagged firms** (Merida litigation overhang, Mainvest
  defunct, High Times Accelerator inactive, SeedInvest merged) without
  first verifying current status.
- **Treat IR contacts at public MSOs as partnership channels, not
  fundraising channels.** They can route you to corp dev when the data
  product is mature — not to write a Series A check.
